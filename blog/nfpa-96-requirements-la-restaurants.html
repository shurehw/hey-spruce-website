What is NFPA 96?

NFPA 96 is the National Fire Protection Association's Standard for Ventilation Control and Fire Protection of Commercial Cooking Operations. In Los Angeles, this standard is enforced by:

  • Los Angeles Fire Department (LAFD)
  • LA County Fire Department (LACoFD)
  • Local city fire marshals
  • Insurance companies
  • Health department inspectors

Complete NFPA 96 System Components

1. Kitchen Hood System Requirements

Hood Construction Standards:

  • Material: 16-gauge stainless steel minimum
  • Overhang: 6" beyond cooking equipment
  • Height: 6'6" minimum clearance
  • Filters: UL-listed baffle or mesh type

Cleaning Requirements:

  • Interior hood surfaces: Down to bare metal
  • Filters: Remove and clean separately
  • Light fixtures: Clean and verify operation
  • Fire suppression nozzles: Clean without adjustment

2. Exhaust Ductwork Standards

Construction Requirements:

  • Material: 16-gauge steel (18-gauge for round ducts)
  • Joints: Welded or locked with sealant
  • Slope: Minimum 1/4" per foot toward hood
  • Access panels: Every 20 feet maximum

Cleaning Specifications:

  • Horizontal ducts: Complete interior cleaning
  • Vertical ducts: Full length cleaning
  • Access panels: Open, clean, reseal properly
  • Maximum residue: 0.002" (bare metal visible)

3. Exhaust Fan Requirements

Maintenance Standards:

  • Fan wheel: Clean to bare metal
  • Fan housing: Complete interior cleaning
  • Belt drives: Inspect and adjust tension
  • Electrical components: Check and secure connections
  • Vibration: Test and document levels

Los Angeles Fire Department Inspection Process

What LAFD Inspectors Check:

  1. Documentation Review (First Thing Checked)

    • Current cleaning certificates
    • Proper certification numbers
    • Complete service history
    • Insurance compliance letters

  2. Visual Hood Inspection

    • Grease accumulation levels
    • Filter condition and fit
    • Light operation
    • Visible damage or modifications

  3. Ductwork Assessment

    • Access panel inspection (may open randomly)
    • Grease drip evidence
    • Proper slope verification
    • Joint integrity

  4. Fan and Roof Inspection

    • Fan operation test
    • Grease accumulation on roof
    • Proper discharge direction
    • Safety equipment (guards, etc.)

  5. Fire Suppression System

    • Current certification tags
    • Nozzle positioning
    • Manual pull stations
    • Fusible links condition

Special Considerations for Different Cuisine Types

Asian Restaurants (Chinese, Korean, Thai)

Challenges:

  • High-heat wok cooking creates heavy grease
  • Oils vaporize quickly at high temperatures
  • Seasoning and spices increase residue

NFPA 96 Recommendation:

Monthly cleaning regardless of volume

Mexican Restaurants

Challenges:

  • Grilling and charring create carbon buildup
  • Cheese and proteins leave stubborn residue
  • High-volume typically

NFPA 96 Recommendation:

Monthly to quarterly based on grill use

Mediterranean (Greek, Middle Eastern)

Challenges:

  • Vertical rotisseries (gyro, shawarma)
  • Open-flame grilling
  • Heavy oil use in cooking

NFPA 96 Recommendation:

Quarterly minimum, monthly if high volume

American/Burger Restaurants

Challenges:

  • Char-broiling creates heavy grease
  • Bacon cooking adds significant residue
  • Deep frying compounds buildup

NFPA 96 Recommendation:

Monthly for char-broilers, quarterly for others

Emergency Preparedness and NFPA 96

Fire Emergency Procedures:

  1. Activate fire suppression: Pull manual station
  2. Turn off equipment: Gas and electric shutoffs
  3. Evacuate: Staff and customers immediately
  4. Call 911: Report exact location and situation
  5. Do not re-enter: Until fire department clears

Staff Training Requirements:

  • Location of manual pull stations
  • Gas and electric shutoff procedures
  • Fire extinguisher operation (K-class for kitchens)
  • Evacuation routes and assembly points
  • Weekly filter cleaning procedures
