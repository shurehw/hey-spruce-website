Multi-location QA that scales: checklists, photo evidence, exception logs, and scorecards

If QA lives in texts, emails, and "looks good" drive-bys, it won't scale. Multi-location ops need a single system that turns every visit into documented outputs, and every miss into a tracked exception with ownership and closure.

The minimum viable QA loop

  • Checklist: define the standard and confirm completion (per location, per scope).
  • Photo evidence: prove completion for the items that matter.
  • Exception log: capture misses as corrective actions (owner + due date).
  • Scorecards: roll up performance by location and vendor so ops can spot repeat issues.

Exception logs: turning misses into owned actions

Exceptions are not "notes." They are work. The rule is simple: if it matters enough to mention, it matters enough to assign, due-date, and close with evidence.

  • Write the exception like a ticket: what, where, and the constraint (access, equipment not movable, missing parts).
  • Assign an owner: vendor, ops, maintenance, or manager.
  • Set a due date: even if it's a placeholder pending parts -- no due date means no priority.
  • Define closure evidence: what proof is required to mark it closed (photos, re-checklist, certificate).
  • Mark repeats explicitly: repeated exceptions are a leadership signal, not a cleaning task.

Monthly scorecards: what leadership actually reviews

Leadership doesn't review 1,000 photos. They review a scorecard that shows whether execution is happening and where the system is failing. If you can't roll QA up to a few ops metrics, it won't stay funded.

  • Completion % tells whether scheduled work is closing as planned.
  • Rework % tells whether "closed" really meant "done."
  • Repeat exceptions tells where the same issues recur (location, vendor, or scope problem).
  • Median time-to-close tells whether issues are being resolved or just logged.
  • SLA adherence (per agreement) tells whether response/closure targets are being met by priority class.
