Inspection readiness without promises: corrective actions + evidence + closure

Inspection readiness is an operations workflow, not a claim. The only defensible approach is to remediate the actual issues, document what changed, and close the loop with evidence you can retrieve on demand.

From inspection risk to controlled workflow

When inspection risk spikes, most teams scramble. A controlled workflow looks like this:

  1. Open a priority work order with scope, owner, and time window.
  2. Run a targeted checklist aligned to the specific risk areas (BOH, FOH, hood/docs, storage).
  3. Capture evidence for completion (photos, logs, certificates).
  4. Log exceptions as corrective actions with owners and due dates.
  5. Close only with evidence and keep the package retrievable.

This is the operational pattern GroundOps runs through the platform and vendor management so readiness is auditable across locations.

Corrective action logs: ownership and closure

If an issue doesn't have an owner and due date, it's not being managed. A corrective action log is where exceptions go to become owned work with closure requirements.

Example artifact: corrective action log excerpt (redacted)

Location Corrective action Owner Due Closure evidence Status
Location 2 Drain area buildup requires pull-out + re-checklist Ops -- Before/after photos + checklist re-run Dispatched
Location 4 Missing hood documentation for last service (NFPA records) Vendor -- Certificate uploaded + record linked Open
Location 1 Recurring grease behind equipment (access constraint documented) Vendor -- Photo set + exception marked "repeat" Open

Evidence required to pass repeat inspections

Evidence isn't "nice to have." It's what prevents the same exception from reappearing as "he said/she said." The evidence package should be consistent and retrievable.

Example artifact: evidence checklist fields

Evidence item Required when Stored as
Checklist completion Every visit Checklist record linked to work order
Photo evidence (key risk areas) Deep cleans, inspection readiness, repeat exceptions Timestamped photos attached to work order
Exception notes + corrective action owner Any miss or constraint Exception log entry with owner + due date
NFPA / compliance certificate (if applicable) Hood/exhaust documentation needs Certificate stored and linked to location record
Closure confirmation When corrective action is resolved Closed status + evidence required to close
