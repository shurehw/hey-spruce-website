The Ultimate Restaurant Cleaning Checklist for 2024

Managing a restaurant in Los Angeles means juggling countless tasks, but none is more critical than maintaining a clean, auditable standard. This restaurant cleaning checklist is designed to help you standardize execution, assign ownership, and document completion across shifts and locations.

Why this checklist works for LA restaurants

  • Built around inspection risk areas -- aligned to common LA County findings
  • Prevents common health violations -- addresses most common infractions
  • Efficient task organization -- streamlined by frequency
  • Digital and print ready -- use on tablets or laminate for kitchen use
  • Customizable -- adapt to your specific restaurant needs
  • Staff accountability -- built-in signature lines and timestamps

Failed inspection emergency clean

If you receive violations, complete these immediately:

  1. Address critical violations first (red items)
  2. Document everything with photos
  3. Complete this checklist:
    • Fix all temperature violations
    • Clean and sanitize all food contact surfaces
    • Ensure proper hand washing stations
    • Remove any pest evidence
    • Fix any plumbing issues
    • Clean floors and walls completely
    • Organize and date all food storage
    • Update all required signage
    • Prepare documentation for re-inspection

Equipment-specific cleaning procedures

Deep fryer boil-out procedure

  1. Turn off fryer and let cool to 150F
  2. Drain oil completely (dispose properly)
  3. Remove and clean baskets separately
  4. Fill with water to normal oil level
  5. Add fryer cleaner per instructions
  6. Bring to slow boil for 20 minutes
  7. Turn off and let cool
  8. Drain and rinse thoroughly
  9. Wipe dry with clean towels
  10. Refill with fresh oil

Flat-top grill cleaning

  1. Turn off and let cool to 150F
  2. Scrape surface with grill scraper
  3. Apply grill cleaner or oil
  4. Scrub with grill brick in circular motion
  5. Wipe clean with damp cloth
  6. Apply thin layer of oil to season
  7. Clean grease traps underneath
  8. Wipe down exterior surfaces

Ice machine monthly cleaning

  1. Turn off machine and empty ice
  2. Remove and wash parts per manual
  3. Mix approved ice machine cleaner
  4. Run cleaning cycle
  5. Rinse thoroughly
  6. Sanitize with approved sanitizer
  7. Run 2-3 batches and discard
  8. Return to normal operation

Documentation and record keeping

Required logs and records

Los Angeles County requires these records be kept on-site and available for inspection:

  • Temperature Logs: Daily, keep for 6 months
  • Cleaning Schedules: Signed and dated
  • Hood Cleaning Certificates: Keep for 3 years
  • Pest Control Reports: Monthly, keep for 1 year
  • Equipment Maintenance: All service records
  • Employee Training: Signed acknowledgments
  • Chemical MSDS Sheets: Current versions
  • Waste Oil Disposal: Receipts and manifests
  • Grease Trap Service: Pumping records
  • Fire Suppression Tests: Semi-annual certificates

Los Angeles specific requirements

LA County special considerations

  • Outdoor Dining Areas: Must be cleaned daily, pressure washed weekly
  • Earthquake Preparedness: Secure all chemical storage, anchor equipment
  • Water Conservation: Use low-flow pre-rinse sprayers, fix leaks immediately
  • Grease Disposal: Must use licensed hauler, keep manifests 2 years
  • Air Quality: Clean hood filters weekly during high pollution days
  • Pest Control: Monthly service required (not quarterly)
  • Food Handler Cards: All staff must have valid cards
  • Allergen Awareness: Separate cleaning tools for allergen-free areas
