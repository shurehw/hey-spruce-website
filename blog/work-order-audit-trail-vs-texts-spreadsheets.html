What replaces texts and spreadsheets: a work order audit trail ops can trust

Texts and spreadsheets work until they don't -- usually right when you need proof: repeat issues, manager turnover, vendor disputes, or inspection risk. A work order audit trail replaces "we think it happened" with ownership, timestamps, evidence, and closure.

The minimum viable work order record

If your "system" can't answer these questions in 30 seconds, it's not a system:

  • What was requested, at which location, and by whom?
  • Who owns it right now (vendor/ops/PM), and what's the due date?
  • What evidence is required to close it?
  • Was it reopened (rework), and why?

Status lifecycle ops can audit

A simple lifecycle is enough, as long as it's enforced:

  1. Opened -- intake captured
  2. Dispatched -- assigned to vendor/crew
  3. In progress -- work underway
  4. Pending evidence -- checklist/photos required
  5. Closed -- evidence attached and verified
  6. Reopened (if needed) -- tracked as rework with reason
