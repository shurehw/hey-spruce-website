Case Studies / Fast-casual group

How a fast-casual group standardized facilities execution across 8 locations

Operational outputs were tracked as work orders with QA evidence and monthly scorecards (redacted examples below). Metrics are measured in-platform; this writeup focuses on the workflow and artifacts.

See the vendor-management model Get an ops walkthrough

Context

  • Locations: 8
  • Operating hours: breakfast through late evening (varies by site)
  • Constraints: high volume, limited after-hours windows, inspection risk, frequent manager turnover

Problems (max 3)

  • Inconsistent vendor execution across locations (standards drifted by manager/site)
  • No audit trail: “done” could not be verified with evidence
  • Repeat exceptions: the same issues reappeared without ownership or closure tracking

System deployed

Intake → work orders → QA checklist → photo evidence → exception log → monthly scorecards.

Intake & triage

Requests route into one queue with owner, scope, and timestamps.

QA evidence

Each visit produces a checklist and photo evidence; exceptions become corrective actions.

Reporting

Monthly scorecards roll up completion, rework, and repeat exceptions by location/vendor.

Operational outputs

Tracked metrics (examples): completion %, rework %, repeat exceptions, median time-to-close, SLA adherence. Numbers are reported in-platform; artifacts below show the format.
