How a full-service restaurant group standardized inspection readiness across 5 locations

The goal was operational: remediate issues, document changes, and prevent repeat exceptions. This case study shows the workflow and artifact formats (redacted), not unverifiable claims.

Context

  • Locations: 5
  • Operating hours: lunch through late night
  • Constraints: limited downtime windows, multiple managers, frequent vendor handoffs

Problems (max 3)

  • Inspection risk was handled reactively (no repeatable corrective-action workflow)
  • No evidence package: checklists/photos were inconsistent or missing
  • Repeat exceptions persisted across locations without ownership and due dates

System deployed

Intake, work orders, QA checklist, photo evidence, exception log, scorecards.

Corrective actions

Exceptions are logged with owners and due dates; closure requires evidence.

Inspection readiness

Documentation is packaged per visit: checklist + photos + exceptions resolved.

Reporting

Monthly scorecards show recurring issues and closure performance by location/vendor.

Operational outputs

Tracked metrics (examples): repeat exceptions by category, closure time distribution, rework frequency, SLA adherence by priority class. Artifacts below show the format.
